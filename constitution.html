Constitution of India

A summary of 30 key points

1. Adoption

The Constitution was adopted on January 26, 1950.

2. Preamble

Establishes the objectives of justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.

3. Sovereignty

Declares India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic.

4. Fundamental Rights

Guarantees six fundamental rights to citizens, ensuring personal freedoms.

5. Right to Equality

Includes equality before law and prohibition of discrimination.

6. Right to Freedom

Guarantees freedom of speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, and profession.

7. Right against Exploitation

Prohibits human trafficking, forced labor, and child labor.

8. Right to Freedom of Religion

Ensures freedom of conscience and the right to practice any religion.

9. Cultural and Educational Rights

Protects the rights of minorities to conserve their culture and establish educational institutions.

10. Right to Constitutional Remedies

Allows individuals to approach the Supreme Court for enforcement of fundamental rights.

11. Fundamental Duties

Lists ten duties for citizens, promoting respect for the Constitution and environment.

12. Directive Principles of State Policy

Guidelines for the state to promote social and economic welfare.

13. Separation of Powers

Establishes distinct executive, legislative, and judicial branches.

14. Parliamentary System

India follows a parliamentary system of governance.

15. President

Acts as the ceremonial head of state, elected by an electoral college.

16. Prime Minister

Head of government, responsible for running the executive branch.

Summary

These points summarize the fundamental framework and principles of the Constitution, reflecting India's commitment to democracy and justice.
